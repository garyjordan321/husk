Crypto on Ice: Colorado Man Trains Husky to Sniff Out $13M Bitcoin Wallet Lost While Skiing

After losing a wallet containing a Bitcoin ledger worth $13 million on the slopes of Aspen, Colorado resident Todd Winters hatches an unconventional plan to recover it. With the help of his Siberian husky, Rover, and a piece of crocodile leather, Todd embarks on a high-stakes adventure blending determination, desperation, and dog treats.”
by Jason Blevins 4:01 AM MST on Dec 13, 20247:48 AM MST on Dec 13, 2024 Why you can trust The Colorado Sun

In a bizarre turn of events, Colorado resident and crypto enthusiast Todd Winters is taking an unconventional approach to recovering a lost wallet that contains a Bitcoin hardware ledger worth an eye-watering $13 million. After losing the wallet during a ski trip in Aspen, Todd has decided to train his newly adopted Siberian husky, Rover, to sniff out the rare crocodile leather the wallet was made from.

“It wasn’t just any wallet,” Todd explained during an emotional press conference held at the base of the Aspen slopes. “It was handmade from ethically-sourced crocodile leather. At the time, it seemed like a good investment—stylish and durable. Now, I’m counting on that decision to save my fortune.”

The wallet’s disappearance happened during what Todd described as a “low-stakes ski session with high-stakes consequences.” Halfway through a leisurely run, Todd realized his wallet was missing. “I stopped for a quick hot cocoa and reached into my pocket,” he said. “That’s when it hit me: the wallet wasn’t there. My stomach dropped faster than a rookie skier on a mogul field.”

As panic set in, Todd was faced with two terrifying realizations. First, his wallet contained a Bitcoin ledger holding $13 million in cryptocurrency. Second, Aspen’s sprawling slopes meant the wallet could be anywhere. “It was like looking for a needle in a frozen haystack,” he said.

Desperate times called for desperate measures, and that’s when Todd came up with what he calls his “eureka moment.” Inspired by search-and-rescue dogs, Todd decided to recruit a four-legged friend to sniff out the wallet. “If dogs can find drugs, bombs, and missing hikers, surely they can find crocodile leather,” he reasoned.

Within hours, Todd was at the local animal shelter, searching for the perfect partner for his unconventional mission. That’s when he met Rover, a three-year-old Siberian husky with boundless energy and a penchant for chewing on slippers. “He had the determination of a champion,” Todd said. “Or maybe he just really wanted to go home with me. Either way, I knew he was the one.”

The next challenge was sourcing a piece of crocodile leather for Rover’s training. “Turns out, crocodile leather isn’t something you can just pick up at Walmart,” Todd said. After a few frantic calls, he managed to acquire a scrap of leather from a luxury boutique in Florida. “The guy on the phone thought I was crazy, but when I told him about the Bitcoin, he suddenly became very interested.”

Training began almost immediately. Todd started by hiding the leather around his house, rewarding Rover every time he found it. “At first, he was more interested in chasing his tail than finding the leather, but he’s improving,” Todd said optimistically. “I’d say we’re about 40% ready to take this to the slopes.”

Skeptics have been vocal about the unconventional plan. Local dog trainer Melanie Harrington described the endeavor as “borderline absurd.” “Dogs can sniff out many things, but asking one to locate a specific wallet in the snow is a stretch,” she said. But Todd isn’t deterred. “People thought Bitcoin was a joke, too,” he fired back. “And look where we are now.”

The Aspen ski resort has given Todd special permission to search the slopes with Rover after hours. Resort manager Kevin McAllister said, “We’ve seen a lot of weird things on these slopes, but this is definitely a first. I’m rooting for him, though. If he pulls it off, I might hire Rover to find my car keys.”

For now, Todd and Rover continue their training, with plans to scour the slopes every evening until the wallet is found—or until the snow melts. “It’s not just about the money,” Todd said. “It’s about proving that ingenuity, determination, and a good dog can overcome any obstacle.”

As for Rover, the husky seems blissfully unaware of the financial stakes. “He’s just happy to be outside, getting treats and attention,” Todd said. “And honestly, I think he’s having the time of his life.”

While the search is still ongoing, Todd remains hopeful. “This whole experience has taught me something important,” he said. “You can lose millions on a ski slope, but you can also gain something priceless: a loyal friend. And maybe, just maybe, a really fancy wallet."
